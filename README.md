# DeepCSAT: E-Commerce Customer Satisfaction Score Prediction

## 📌 Problem Statement & Objective

### 🧩 Problem Statement

In the e-commerce industry, Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) plays a crucial role in determining customer retention, repeat purchases, and overall brand reputation. Traditionally, CSAT is measured using post-interaction surveys. However, this approach has several limitations:

- It is **reactive**, as feedback is collected after the interaction  
- It is often **delayed**, reducing its usefulness for immediate action  
- It is **incomplete**, since many customers do not respond  

At the same time, e-commerce platforms generate large volumes of customer interaction data through channels such as chat, email, and calls. This data contains valuable insights into customer experience but is typically underutilized.

**Core Problem:**

> How can we leverage customer interaction data to predict customer satisfaction proactively instead of relying on delayed survey responses?

---

## 🎯 Project Objective

### ✅ Primary Objective

Develop a **Deep Learning-based Artificial Neural Network (ANN)** model capable of predicting customer satisfaction (CSAT) scores using historical customer interaction data.

### 🔍 Secondary Objectives

- Perform data cleaning and preprocessing  
- Conduct feature engineering on numerical, categorical, and textual data  
- Design and train an Artificial Neural Network model  
- Evaluate model performance using standard metrics (MAE, RMSE, R²)  
- Extract actionable business insights from predictions  
- Demonstrate feasibility of local deployment  

---

## 🚀 Expected Outcome

This project aims to transform CSAT measurement from a **reactive process** into a **proactive, data-driven system**, enabling businesses to:

- Identify dissatisfied customers early  
- Improve service quality in real time  
- Optimize customer support operations  
- Enhance overall customer experience
